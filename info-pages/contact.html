Thank you ever so much for tripping, slipping, or full‑on moonwalking your way onto the contact page for our upcoming documentary, Trial by Media: A Michael Jackson Stan Story. We’re genuinely thrilled by your interest—thrilled in the way only someone who’s just discovered a limited‑edition sparkly glove can be. Feel free to send us any polite, sensible questions… though if you’re here to ask where to buy rhinestone‑encrusted socks, we may need a quiet word.

One of our noble missions is to educate the public about a rather persistent condition we’ve dubbed Michael Jackson glorification disease. And no, it’s not just a cheeky label—it’s as essential to understand as knowing when not to shout “Hee‑hee!” in a crowded Tesco. We’re here to shine a bright, unflattering light on the perils of celebrity worship, because frankly, the world doesn’t need another adult who behaves like they’re starring in Living in the Shadow of a Pop King: The Musical.

Our team is fully prepped to offer all the resources you need to start thinking slightly more rational thoughts about the media and the pop stars we collectively lose our heads over. If you’re feeling a bit faint from excessive idol worship—or if you’ve recently caught yourself wearing a fedora indoors and calling it “a lifestyle”—do get in touch.

Together, we can shuffle, slide, and sidestep our way toward a society that values common sense over full‑blown celebrity hysteria. And while Jesus may not be taking appointments at this time, good judgement and basic decency are still very much open for business. So drop us a line, and let’s try to make sense of this glitter‑soaked, star‑struck circus we call modern fandom.
